## Significance

Palsgraf is the foundational American authority on duty and foreseeability in negligence. Cardozo's majority establishes that duty runs only to plaintiffs within the reasonably foreseeable zone of danger, making foreseeability an element of duty rather than merely proximate cause. Andrews's influential dissent reframes negligence as a duty to the world, resolving liability through proximate-cause analysis. The competing approaches continue to shape modern tort doctrine, jury instructions, and the Restatements.
