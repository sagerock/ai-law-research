Andrews rejected duty limited to foreseeable plaintiffs. > Harm to some one being the natural result of the act, not only that one alone, but all those in fact injured may complain.

## Significance

Palsgraf is foundational for negligence doctrine’s treatment of foreseeability. Cardozo places foreseeability within duty, limiting protection to plaintiffs within the apparent zone of risk; Andrews places it principally within proximate cause, after recognizing a broader duty to society. The opinions frame an enduring debate over whether unforeseeable-plaintiff cases should be resolved categorically by judges as duty questions or through flexible, policy-sensitive causal analysis.
